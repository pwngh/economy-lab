A collab sells: an artist and an animator split the price, and the platform keeps its cut. That is
one [`spend`](/economy/reference/operations/spend/) with several `recipients` — never several
transfers to reconcile after the fact.

The split rule is owned by the [pricing policy](/economy/ports/pricing/) and runs inside the sale's
one balanced transaction:

1. The platform's fee comes off the top.
2. Each recipient takes their `shareBps` of the net — basis points, and the shares must sum to
   exactly `10_000`, so no share of the price can go unclaimed.
3. Whatever rounding leaves over goes to the house, not into thin air: the books stay balanced to
   the minor unit.

<Runnable name="recipe-fee-split" html={recipeFeeSplitHtml} />

Each seller's cut lands in their [`earned` balance](/economy/concepts/accounts-and-double-entry/),
which is the balance [`requestPayout`](/economy/reference/operations/request-payout/) later pays
out as real money. A [`refund`](/economy/reference/operations/refund/) of the order unwinds every
share — both sellers and the fee — in one opposite posting.
