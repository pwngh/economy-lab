A monthly allowance, a daily login grant, a weekly stipend — every recurring credit is the same
recipe: any timer you already have, plus an idempotency key minted from the period instead of from
the moment.

The timer is deliberately not the interesting part. A cron job, a queue with a delay, or the same
[`Scheduler`](/economy/reference/background-worker/) seam the background worker runs on all work,
because the safety lives in the key: fire twice in one period and the second submit is a
[`duplicate`](/economy/reference/outcomes-and-reason-codes/), not a second grant.

<Runnable name="recipe-allowance" html={recipeAllowanceHtml} />

The key discipline is the whole trick, and it is the same one every
[idempotent retry](/economy/concepts/idempotency/) uses: derive the key from the action's own
identity (`allowance_usr_kid_2026-07`), never from `Date.now()` or a random id at fire time. A
timer that misfires, a job that retries after a crash, and a deploy that replays a backlog all
collapse into the one grant the period was owed.
